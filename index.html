What is Wallet Recovery?

Cryptocurrency wallet recovery is the process of restoring access to a digital wallet when the owner has lost their private key, seed phrase, wallet file, or password. Unlike traditional banks, blockchain wallets have no central authority — if you lose access, there's no "forgot password" button. This is where our forensic specialists come in.

Common Scenarios We Handle

  • Lost or forgotten seed phrase — Including partial reconstruction using cryptographic permutation methods
  • Corrupted or damaged wallet files — wallet.dat corruption, partial file recovery
  • Forgotten wallet passwords — Advanced decryption for encrypted wallet files
  • Old or abandoned wallets — Wallets created years ago with lost credentials
  • Deleted wallet applications — Forensic data recovery from device storage

Supported Networks

Bitcoin, Ethereum, Solana, Cardano, Polygon, Avalanche, BNB Chain, and 60+ others. All major wallet clients supported including MetaMask, Exodus, Electrum, Trust Wallet, and more.

How Long Does It Take?

Simple password recovery: 24–72 hours. Complex cases involving partial seed phrases or corrupted files: 5–14 business days. Regular status updates throughout.

🔐 SIM-Swap Attack Prevention

Security · March 2026 · 8 min read

SIM-swap fraud has surged over 300% in 2025–2026. Attackers specifically target crypto holders using social engineering against mobile carriers.

What is a SIM-Swap Attack?

An attacker convinces your mobile carrier to transfer your phone number to a SIM they control. With your number, they bypass SMS-based 2FA on exchange accounts, email, and other services — enabling full account takeover within minutes.

Defensive Steps That Actually Work

  • Set a SIM Lock PIN with Your Carrier — Mandatory PIN required for any account changes
  • Switch to App-Based 2FA — Replace SMS 2FA with Google Authenticator or a YubiKey
  • Use a Dedicated Email for Crypto — Secured with a hardware key, used only for exchange accounts
  • Remove Your Phone Number from Exchange Accounts — Use authenticator apps instead of SMS

📜 Partial Seed Phrase Recovery

Recovery · February 2026 · 10 min read

Losing even a few words of your seed phrase doesn't necessarily mean permanent loss. With enough known words, cryptographic reconstruction may be possible.

How Partial Recovery Works

BIP39 seed phrases include a checksum — a mathematical verification mechanism. This means not all word combinations are valid, dramatically reducing combinations to test when some words are missing.

  • 12-word phrase, 1 missing word — 2,048 combinations. Recoverable in seconds.
  • 12-word phrase, 2 missing words — ~4 million combinations. Minutes with our GPU cluster.
  • 24-word phrase, 3 missing words — ~8.5 billion valid combinations. Hours to days.

Warning

Never enter your partial seed phrase into any online tool. Scammers specifically target people who have lost seed phrase words. Always verify any recovery service before sharing wallet information.

💾 Damaged Hardware Wallet Recovery

Hardware · December 2025 · 9 min read

Physical destruction of a hardware wallet does not necessarily mean permanent loss. We have recovered assets from devices that were underwater, in fires, and physically crushed.

Water Damage — Act Immediately

DO NOT attempt to power it on. Remove from water, dry the exterior gently, place in silica gel packets. Contact us immediately — water-damaged devices have ~75% recovery success when they reach us without being powered on.

Fire or Heat Damage

Secure element chips in hardware wallets can survive temperatures up to 400°C in many cases. Our lab performs chip-off extraction — physically removing the storage chip from the damaged PCB and reading it directly.

Chip-Off Process

Performed in our ISO-certified cleanroom: non-destructive assessment → micro-soldering chip extraction → custom interface adapter → raw flash memory read → cryptographic key reconstruction.

🎣 Crypto Phishing in 2026

Fraud · November 2025 · 6 min read

In 2025, phishing attacks accounted for over $2.3 billion in crypto losses globally. AI-generated content makes many attacks nearly indistinguishable from legitimate communications.

New Attack Types in 2026

  • Spear Phishing — Personalised messages using data breach info, mimicking your exchange
  • Fake Wallet Popups — Browser extensions injecting fake wallet connection dialogs into DeFi sites
  • AI Deepfake Video Calls — Impersonating exchange reps or financial advisors to extract seed phrases
  • Discord & Telegram Impersonation — Fake admins offering "wallet sync fixes"

How to Protect Yourself

  • Never enter your seed phrase anywhere online — no legitimate service ever needs it
  • Bookmark exchange URLs and only access from bookmarks, never email links
  • Enable hardware key (YubiKey) authentication on all exchange accounts
  • If something feels off — stop and verify independently before proceeding
